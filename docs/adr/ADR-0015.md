# ADR-0015 — Native Chain First; ERC-20 Deprecated as Primary Goal

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** July 2026
**Replaces:** Initial plan to deploy ERC-20 token as primary fundraising vehicle

---

## Context

The initial ABYSS roadmap included deploying an ERC-20 token on Ethereum
as a temporary placeholder for AC during the fundraising phase,
with a planned 1:1 migration to the native chain at mainnet genesis.

During early development, as the Rust core (`abyss-core`, `abyss-consensus`,
`abyss-tokenomics`, `abyss-wallet`, `abyss-social`) took shape, a clearer
picture emerged: the project's technical foundation is strong enough to
pursue the native chain directly.

The ERC-20 approach carries structural costs that compound over time:

1. **Two systems to maintain**: an ERC-20 contract on Ethereum and the native
   AC ledger are two separate systems, each with its own security surface.

2. **Migration risk**: a 1:1 ERC-20 → native migration requires a bridge
   contract that must be audited, operated, and eventually deprecated.
   Every migration is a moment of maximum risk.

3. **Misaligned narrative**: a project whose core proposition is an
   independent, private, decentralised chain is weakened by being seen
   as an Ethereum token. Investors and users who understand blockchain
   architecture recognise the difference.

4. **Incentive drift**: once an ERC-20 is liquid, pressure builds to
   maintain ERC-20 compatibility indefinitely rather than migrate.
   This has happened to many projects that intended ERC-20 as temporary.

5. **Architecture before features** (ADR-0007): the ERC-20 is a feature
   workaround. Building the native chain is the architectural path.

---

## Decision

> **The primary goal of ABYSS is the native chain and native AC coin.
> ERC-20 is not the primary objective and will not be pursued as such.**

Specific consequences:

- Development effort focuses on `abyss-core`, `abyss-consensus`,
  and the path to a multi-validator testnet (Phase 3 in ROADMAP.md).
- If external ecosystem compatibility is needed in the future, it will
  be implemented through **bridges** and **wrapped tokens** (e.g. wAC on
  Ethereum), not by making ERC-20 the canonical form of AC.
- The sale structure (Investor Secondary Window, DEX Final Sale) proceeds
  as documented in ADR-0013 and ADR-0012, but settlement is deferred to
  the native chain rather than an ERC-20 contract.
- The whitepaper Section 8.4 ("ERC-20 Bridge Period and Migration") is
  superseded by this decision. Future whitepaper versions will reflect
  a native-first model.

---

## Consequences

**Advantages:**
- One system, one security surface, one set of keys to protect.
- No migration event — investors receive native AC directly at genesis.
- The project's narrative is architecturally consistent:
  "We built our own chain" is stronger than "We had an ERC-20 and migrated."
- Development pressure is focused on the right problem: the chain itself.

**Trade-offs:**
- Fundraising before mainnet requires managing investor allocations
  off-chain (signed agreements + registry) rather than through
  a deployed ERC-20 contract.
- Investors cannot see a live token on Etherscan during the pre-mainnet period.
  This is mitigated by the working devnet, the whitepaper, and
  the documented tokenomics model (which is already implemented and tested).

**Bridges and wrapped tokens (future):**
When cross-chain compatibility with Ethereum, BSC, or other EVM chains
becomes a product requirement, it will be delivered as:
- A bridge contract on the external chain
- A wrapped AC (wAC) token representing locked native AC
- A bridge operator or decentralised bridge protocol

This is strictly additive — it extends ABYSS without replacing its economy.

---

## Notes

This decision aligns ABYSS with the approach taken by the most credible
independent chains: Bitcoin, Monero, and Zcash raised investment and built
communities before having a liquid ERC-20 representation.
Their native coins are the canonical asset. Bridge representations exist
where needed but are not the product.
