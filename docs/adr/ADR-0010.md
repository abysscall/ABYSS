# ADR-0010 — Privacy by Default

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2025

## Context

Most privacy-enabled blockchains treat privacy as opt-in.

Zcash, the most prominent example, has two transaction types: transparent (like Bitcoin)
and shielded. In practice, the vast majority of ZEC transactions are transparent.
This creates a small anonymity set for shielded transactions, making statistical
deanonymisation easier.

An opt-in privacy model has a structural flaw: the users who most need privacy
(those conducting sensitive transactions) are distinguishable from the users
who did not opt in, by the very act of opting in.

## Decision

All standard AC transfers in ABYSS are shielded by default:

- Sender, receiver, and amount are encrypted on-chain
- Validated via zero-knowledge proof, not disclosed in plaintext
- No unshielded transaction type for standard transfers

Additional privacy mechanisms:
- **Stealth addresses**: every transaction uses a one-time derived address
- **Private mempool**: pending transactions are not visible before block inclusion
- **Selective disclosure**: view keys allow chosen third parties to see specific transactions
- **Multi-prover ZK**: verification through ≥2 independent proof backends (Phase 4)

## Consequences

- All users contribute to the anonymity set, not just those who opt in
- Stealth addresses prevent address-clustering analysis
- View keys satisfy compliance requirements without compromising network privacy
- Private mempool prevents front-running and MEV extraction
- ZK circuit implementation is the largest engineering cost (Phase 4)
