# ADR-0006 — Replaceable Cryptographic Algorithms

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026

---

## Context

-e Development currently uses placeholder hashing in abyss-crypto. Production cryptography will be more demanding: SHA-256 or BLAKE3 for general hashing, Groth16 and PLONK for ZK proofs, EdDSA or BLS for validator signatures. Cryptographic best practices evolve; algorithms that are secure today may require replacement tomorrow. Hardcoding specific algorithms into business logic makes future upgrades costly and error-prone.

---

## Decision

-e Business logic in abyss-core and other crates must never depend directly on specific cryptographic algorithms.

All cryptographic primitives are abstracted behind dedicated interfaces in abyss-crypto.

The hashing module exposes:
 fn hash(data: &[u8]) -> Hash256

Not:
 fn sha256(data: &[u8]) -> [u8; 32]

The implementation behind the interface can be swapped without touching callers.

---

## Consequences

-e Future migrations become significantly easier.
The multi-prover ZK design (see ADR-0009) depends on this abstraction.

Trade-off: one additional indirection layer. Acceptable given the upgrade risk it mitigates.
