# ADR-0000 — The Architecture Is the Product

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026

---

## Context

Every technical project faces a recurring pressure: ship faster by
cutting architectural corners. The pressure feels rational because
features are visible and architecture is not.

In blockchain systems this pressure is especially dangerous. A privacy
protocol with a weak architecture is not a slow privacy protocol —
it is a broken one. An architectural mistake at the foundation of a
chain cannot be patched without a hard fork.

ABYSS needed a guiding principle that would resist this pressure
at the decision level, before individual trade-offs arise.

---

## Decision

> **The architecture is the product.**

ABYSS is not built around features. ABYSS is built around architecture.
Features are consequences of architecture.

This means:

- A feature that fits the architecture is welcome.
- A feature that requires compromising the architecture is deferred
  until an architectural path is found, or rejected.
- The question "How do we add this?" is always preceded by
  "Does this belong here?"

This ADR is numbered 0000 — before all others — because it governs
how all other decisions are made.

---

## Consequences

**Advantages:**
- Prevents short-term pressure from producing long-term structural damage.
- Creates a shared language for declining requests ("it doesn't fit the architecture").
- Makes the codebase more navigable: every subsystem has a reason to exist.

**Trade-offs:**
- Some features take longer to ship.
- Some investor or user expectations must be managed differently.
- The team must be willing to say "not yet" without saying "never".

---

## Notes

This principle was first articulated during early ABYSS development
as a response to the observation that many blockchain projects ship
token sales before having a coherent technical foundation.

The whitepaper (Section 7) reflects this principle by documenting
what is implemented and what is not, rather than presenting aspiration
as fact.
