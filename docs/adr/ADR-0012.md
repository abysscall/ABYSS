# ADR-0012 — Tokenomics and Sale Structure

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2025

## Context

ABYSS requires capital to fund blockchain development (estimated Phase 2–4: significant).
The token must be issued before the native chain exists.

Two concerns arise:

1. **Investor trust**: issuing a token for a chain that does not yet exist
   is high-risk for investors. This is mitigated by demonstrating working
   devnet code, a published whitepaper, and a clear vesting structure.

2. **Token role**: AC is intended as a utility token (fees, staking, governance,
   DEX settlement) — not a security. Sale structure must reflect this.

## Decision

**Supply**: 55,000,000 AC hard cap. No inflation mechanism.

**Split**:
- Team Reserve: 30,000,000 AC (vested, see below)
- Public Sale: 25,000,000 AC (across 5 standard rounds + secondary window + DEX final sale)

**Sale stages** (standard rounds):
| Stage | Tokens | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Sale to Investors | 2,000,000 | $1.00 |
| Pre-Sale | 3,000,000 | $2.00 |
| Sale Stage 1 | 5,000,000 | $3.00 |
| Sale Stage 2 | 5,000,000 | $4.00 |
| Sale Stage 3 | 10,000,000 | $5.00 |

**Team vesting**:
- Tranche A: 10,000,000 AC over 12 months, linear, no cliff
- Tranche B: 20,000,000 AC over 48 months, capped at 5,000,000 AC/year

**ERC-20 bridge period**: AC is issued as a temporary ERC-20 on Ethereum
for the sale period. At mainnet genesis, ERC-20 AC is migrated 1:1 to native AC.
Tokens are non-transferable during the bridge period.

## Consequences

- Early investors get the lowest price; later rounds reflect growing development progress
- Team vesting aligns incentives over 5 years
- ERC-20 bridge is temporary and explicitly documented as such
- Migration contract must be audited before any funds are accepted
