# ADR-0013 — Investor Secondary Window (replaces Buyback)

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2025
**Replaces:** Initial design that included a team buyback obligation

## Context

The original design included an "Investor Buyback" mechanism: after Sale Stage 1,
the ABYSS team would repurchase tokens from Stage I investors at $3.00.

This created a **direct financial obligation on ABYSS** — if Stage I investors
wanted to exit, ABYSS was required to provide liquidity from its own funds.

Problems with this model:

1. **Legal risk**: a guaranteed repurchase obligation resembles a put option,
   which may constitute a security in some jurisdictions.
2. **Financial risk**: if many investors exercise the option simultaneously,
   ABYSS must hold sufficient reserves, diverting capital from development.
3. **Misaligned incentives**: ABYSS bears the risk of investor exit decisions
   it has no control over.

## Decision

Replace the buyback with an **Investor Secondary Window**:

- **Phase A (Registration, 14 days)**: Stage I investors who wish to exit
  submit a listing intent. Minimum listing: 50% of their allocation (≥ 250,000 AC).
- **Phase B (Sales, until sold)**: any participant may purchase listed tokens
  at the fixed price of $3.00/AC.
- **ABYSS role**: facilitator only. ABYSS maintains the listing registry and
  confirms transfers. It has **no obligation to purchase** any tokens.
- **Unsold tokens**: remain with the original holder if Phase B closes with
  tokens still listed.

**Seller economics**: Stage I investors entered at $1.00 and can exit at $3.00 —
a 3× return — without ABYSS bearing the counterparty risk.

## Consequences

- ABYSS has no financial obligation arising from investor exit decisions
- Legal exposure is reduced (facilitated P2P market, not a put option)
- Stage I investors retain a clear, valued exit mechanism
- New participants gain access to Stage I tokens at a fair price ($3.00)
- The mechanism is implemented and tested in `abyss-tokenomics` (SecondaryListing, InvestorSecondaryWindow)
