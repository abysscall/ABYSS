# ADR-0014 — Decentralised Social Layer

**Status:** Accepted (data model implemented; storage deferred)
**Date:** 2025

## Context

Mainstream social platforms are incompatible with the privacy and decentralisation
pillars of ABYSS (ADR-0010):

- User content is stored on centralised servers
- Behavioural data is monetised by the platform operator
- Accounts can be suspended or shadow-banned unilaterally
- Moderation rules are opaque and enforced without appeal

Building a social layer on top of ABYSS is not a product decision bolted on
after the protocol was designed — it is a direct consequence of the account
model. If an account can transact privately, the same account and the same
privacy primitives can carry a social identity.

## Decision

The ABYSS social layer is a fourth protocol pillar (alongside Privacy,
Decentralisation, and AI-Native accounts):

**Identity**: reuses the ABYSS wallet address and stealth-address mechanism.
No separate social account to register, link, or leak.

**Storage**: content-addressed, replicated storage — no company server.
No single party can unilaterally delete published content.

**Visibility**: the same view-key model used for financial transactions
extends to social authorship. A post can be attributed or shielded.

**Moderation**: transparent, on-chain community governance. No opaque
corporate policy team.

**Agent participation**: a personal AI Agent may act in the social layer
under its `AgentSocialPolicy` — separate from and non-escalatable to
financial permissions (see ADR-0011).

**Current implementation** (`abyss-social`):
- `Post`, `Visibility`, `DevFeed`
- `ViewKeyRegistry` — selective authorship disclosure
- `AgentSocialPolicy`, `AgentActivityWindow` — rate-limited agent actions
- 14 unit tests; devnet CLI demo

**Deferred to Phase 6**:
- Content-addressed P2P storage and replication
- On-chain moderation governance
- Creator monetisation mechanics
- Full social graph (follows, reactions)

## Consequences

- The social layer shares the identity and privacy model with the financial layer
- No new trust assumptions are introduced
- Storage and replication are the hardest engineering problems and are explicitly deferred
- The data model is stable and tested, enabling Phase 6 implementation to proceed cleanly
